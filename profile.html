Insights

What's the biggest lesson you've learned as an entrepreneur?

I have two: the first is that you start off knowing absolutely nothing, and you learn a little more every day. Entrepreneurship is a career path of its own, and you get better at it over time.

Second, when you begin to build a company, you should have a clear path to early user adoption. It could be one-on-one meetings, hiring a sales team, viral growth, or something different. It shouldn't be sexy or seem like a silver bullet--it shouldn't scale at first. But you have to figure out as quickly as possible how people want to use what you've built.

"The great thing about building new products is that you put something into the world where previously there was nothing. You can change the world just a little bit. That's damn cool.""

Which products are you most proud of shipping?

Statlas, which @MichaelDeal and I conceived together and launched into the wild during the 2013 All-Star Game. We manually hacked a prototype together during the 2013 MLB playoffs which attracted @GeoffBeck to the project. We built a prototype and were a finalist for YC W14, but ultimately weren't funded.

What are you working on these days?

@MichaelDeal and I are the team behind Caravan. Our mission is to help entrepreneurs, founders, and innovators tell their story and find new opportunities.

Tell us about a time that you failed or struggled.

Though I'm most proud of Statlas, it's also one of my biggest regrets. I managed our team, product, and marketing horribly and a combination of lack of traction and internal strife led to our team disbanding and the product never being completed.

What's the hardest thing you did this week?
